Encampments & SDoH (San Diego) — Thesis context

This page situates the encampments project within my thesis work on the relationship between social determinants of health and the spatial distribution of homelessness in San Diego — using privacy-preserving, tract-level aggregation.

Research framing

Core question: how do tract-level socioeconomic conditions (ACS proxies for SDoH) relate to tract-level encampment presence/volume over time?

  • Unit of analysis: Census tract (protects anonymity; no point locations)
  • Exposure/features: ACS 5-year estimates (income, poverty, rent burden, etc.)
  • Outcome: tract-aggregated encampment counts (or rate, if normalized)
  • Goal: analysis-ready table for descriptives + statistical modeling

Privacy + safety note

Encampments are already aggregated by census tract to ensure safety and anonymity. This workflow intentionally avoids publishing point-level locations or revealing sensitive detail.

Data + join logic (high-level)

  • Join key: tract identifier (typically GEOID)
  • Encampments table: GEOID + encampment_count (+ time fields if used)
  • ACS table: GEOID + selected SDoH indicators (transformed/cleaned)
  • Output: one row per tract (per year, if time series), ready for descriptives and models
